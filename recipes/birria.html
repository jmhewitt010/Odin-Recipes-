Birria Tacos

Birria tacos are a rich, flavorful Mexican dish made with stewed meat, crispy tortillas, and melty cheese.
This recipe will guide you through making the meat, consomé, and assembling the tacos.

Ingredients

For the Birria Stew:

  • 3 lbs beef chuck roast or short ribs
  • 2 dried guajillo chiles
  • 2 dried ancho chiles
  • 1 dried pasilla chile
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 white onion, quartered
  • 1 tbsp cumin
  • 1 tbsp oregano
  • 1/2 tsp ground cloves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar

For the Tacos:

  • Corn tortillas
  • Shredded mozzarella or Oaxaca cheese
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Diced onion
  • Lime wedges

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chiles

Remove stems and seeds from dried chiles.
Toast them lightly in a dry pan for 1–2 minutes.
Soak in hot water for 15 minutes to soften.

Step 2: Make the Sauce

In a blender, combine softened chiles, garlic, onion, cumin, oregano, cloves, vinegar, and 1 cup of broth.
Blend until smooth.

Step 3: Cook the Meat

Sear the meat in a large pot until browned.
Pour in the chile sauce and remaining broth.
Cover and simmer on low for 3–4 hours, or until meat is tender and shreds easily.

Step 4: Shred and Strain

Remove meat, shred it, and return to the pot.
Strain sauce (optional) to remove chunks for a smooth consomé.

Step 5: Assemble the Tacos

Dip tortillas into the top layer of the stew (oil and broth).
Fry on a skillet. Add cheese, shredded meat, and toppings.
Fold and cook until crispy.

Serving

Serve tacos hot with a small bowl of consomé for dipping.
Garnish with lime, cilantro, and diced onion.

Enjoy your delicious, homemade Birria Tacos!
